# Legal Standards & Definitions

Understanding these legal concepts is fundamental to proper law enforcement and ensuring your actions are constitutional and admissible in court.

## REASONABLE SUSPICION

Reasonable Suspicion is the legal standard where a law enforcement officer has specific, articulable facts that suggest a person may be involved in criminal activity.

**Required For:** Terry Stops, Brief Detainment, Pat-Down Searches (if officer believes subject is armed and dangerous)

## PROBABLE CAUSE

Probable Cause is the reasonable belief, based on facts and circumstances, that a crime has been, is being, or will be committed.

**Required For:** Arrests, Search Warrants, Warrantless Searches (under exceptions), Seizure of Evidence

## DETAIN

A detention is a brief holding for investigation based on Reasonable Suspicion.

Duration MUST NOT be longer than reasonably required. Person is not free to leave.

## ARREST

An arrest requires Probable Cause. Subject is taken into custody for charges.

Miranda warnings required before custodial interrogation. Search incident to arrest is permitted.

## MIRANDA RIGHTS

Constitutional warnings required before custodial interrogation:

1.  Right to remain silent
2.  Anything said can be used in court
3.  Right to an attorney
4.  One will be appointed if cannot afford

## PLAIN VIEW DOCTRINE

Officers can seize evidence without warrant when clearly visible during lawful observation.

Officer must be lawfully present, evidence in plain view, incriminating nature immediately apparent.
