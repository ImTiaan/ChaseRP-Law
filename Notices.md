## STATUTE – The Fines to Mines Act

**THE FINES TO MINES ACT – 01/30/2026**

This act establishes criminal penalties for failure to pay fines, as well as grace periods for repayment.

### The San Andreas Correctional Labor Quarry

* Outstanding criminal fines may be repaid through supervised labor at the San Andreas Correctional Labor Quarry. This labor will forgive criminal fines permanently.
* The camp shall ONLY operate while supervised by State Law Enforcement and Corrections Officers.
* Entering the facility while it is not under operations or without approval shall be considered trespassing.
* The Department of Corrections and Law Enforcement may allow detainees to participate in labor during their normal sentences if they wish.
* The camp is open to any person to visit while personnel are available to repay fines through service.
* A person may volunteer as much time as they wish while it is open.

### Mandatory Labor

* Persons who owe $2,000 or more in fines, where the oldest fine is older than a week, may be compelled into mandatory labor. Persons with excessive fines may be located and detained for mandatory labor.
* Choosing to do Labor during a normal prison sentence at Bolingbroke is VOLUNTARY, and is not a part of Mandatory Labor.

### Criminal Penalties for Fines

* A prison sentence for any crime requiring remanding to state prison will have the sentence extended by 10 months per every $5,000 in outstanding fines.

### Grace Period

* Mandatory Labor and Extended Sentences shall begin to apply on February 7th, 2026.
* The Camp will be open before the end of the grace period to allow for fine repayment.

## STATUTE – Treason and Sedition Act

**TREASON AND SEDITION ACT – 1/23/2026**

New Charges added to the Penal Code

**Treason – Felony – HUT**

When any citizen owing allegiance to the State of San Andreas, levies war against San Andreas or adheres to their enemies, giving them aid and comfort within the State or elsewhere. This is a HUT charge.

**Sedition – Felony – HUT**

If two or more persons conspire to overthrow, put down, or destroy by force the San Andreas government, or that branches OR, to use violence or force for the intent of preventing, hindering, or delaying the execution of its laws. OR to use violence or force with intent to seize land. This is a HUT charge.

**Espionage – Felony – HUT**

The illegal gathering, transmitting, or losing of national defense information that could harm the United States or give advantage to a foreign nation. Any person harboring or aiding a person who would be committing the aforementioned acts is also liable to be charged. This is a HUT charge.

## STATUTE – The Fair Campaigns Act

**THE FAIR CAMPAIGNS ACT – 01/01/2026**

This Act shall protect the interests of the citizens of Los Santos, and the power of their vote. The following regulations shall be observed in all elections.

### Section 1 – General Regulations

1. Campaign financing funds may not be transferred between campaigns, even if a campaign has dropped out.
2. No person shall be a part of multiple political organizations that partake in campaign financing or advertising.
3. Campaigns shall not receive donations which will obscure monetary resources or materially paid services to elect an official or influence policy.
4. Campaigns can not be tied to a business entity, and no business entity may raise funds from individuals for political campaigning purposes.
5. Campaigns can not donate to the State or State Agencies.

### Section 2 – Campaign Contributions

1. A Campaign candidate may use their personal funds for campaigning.
2. Any Donation, Expense, Service, or Gift of over $500 must log the name of the organization or individual it was derived from.
3. No campaign or political entity shall receive more than $50,000 from any individual business, or $5,000 from any individual.
4. No business or campaign shall reimburse political donations.
5. No State agency or employee may use the State's funding, or their powers as a State Employee, to aid in a political campaign.
6. All unused campaign funds shall be collected by the Governor's Office at the end of the campaign.
7. No person acting on behalf of the campaign shall be paid by a non-campaign entity for campaign related work.

### Section 3 – Campaign Advertising

1. Any politically funded ad must reveal the campaign, individual, coalition, or business that funded them at some point on the ad. This notice must be clear and conspicuous.
2. Political cartoons and Opinion sections must have the writer(s) named if published through the newspaper or broadcast.
3. All provided advertising timeslots must provide a fair opportunity and equal rates to each political candidate.

## STATUTE – Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act (RICO)

**THE R.I.C.O. ACT – 01/01/2026**

### Section 1 – Prohibited Activities

This Statute prohibits enterprises within the State of San Andreas to have prohibited the following:

1. Enterprises that have invested the proceeds or benefits of racketeering activity into the enterprise OR
2. Enterprises that have acquired or maintained an influence in the enterprise through racketeering activity OR
3. Enterprises that have participated in the affairs of the enterprise via the racketeering activity OR conspired to do one of the preceding

All individuals partaking in the racketeering activity, or having clear knowledge of the activity, may be charged.

### Section 2 – Definitions

1. "Racketeering Activity" is defined as any activity that involves any criminal scheme or operation with an ongoing or recurring profit.
2. An "enterprise" is defined as including any business, union, campaign, or other legal entity. This also includes any union or group of individuals, associated by facts, regardless of legal status.

### Section 3 – Obtaining a Conviction

This statute requires multiple acts of racketeering activity committed to be proven. The acts together must show at least one of the following:

1. The acts must have been committed over a long period of time.
2. The enterprise exists for criminal purposes.
3. The acts are a regular way of conducting the defendant's ongoing business.
4. The acts are a regular way of conducting or participating in any ongoing enterprise.

### Section 4 – Penalties

Each act will be considered for the time and fine imposed, which at minimum shall be 1 year and $100,000 dollars. A RICO charge on a business or individuals may cause the enterprise affected to be dissolved and seized.

## STATUTE – San Andreas Exceptional Defenses

**SAN ANDREAS EXCEPTIONAL DEFENSES – 01/01/2026**

The State of San Andreas recognizes several exceptional defenses to criminal and civil convictions. All other Affirmative Defenses require the defense to prove the affirmation.

### Self Defense

Self Defense is a severe affirmative right and legal justification. Self Defense requires that credible evidence suggests the defendant acted in self defense. Once credible evidence suggests possible self defense, the burden shifts back to the prosecution to prove beyond a reasonable doubt at least 1 aspect of self defense was not met. See State v. Ford.

### The Insanity Defense

Insanity is not an allowed defense within San Andreas. The State of San Andreas may recognize short situations of extreme stress or confusion caused by recent events. This is not insanity, and requires a reasonable person to have acted in such a situation.

## STATUTE – Driver's Licenses

**DRIVER'S LICENSES – 01/01/2026**

Grants ability to drive a motor vehicle on roadways of San Andreas.

### Section 1 – Mandatory Compliance

1. If an officer has probable cause of impairment, the officer may order that a non invasive test, such as a breathalyzer, be taken.

   1. Refusal of a Breathalyzer immediately forfeits an individual's license and the person can be further detained by police to have a blood sample taken for a toxicology report.
2. If the Breathalyzer confirms the probable cause of impairment, a blood sample may be taken for a toxicology report.
3. If an officer has probable cause of impairment by other controlled substances while driving, or a breathalyzer shows impairment by alcohol, the officer may order a blood sample taken for a toxicology report.

### Section 2 – Revocation

1. Officers may revoke licenses immediately if the driver is caught Driving while Intoxicated. The driver is considered driving under the influence of alcohol when they are over the legal limit. If a driver has a BAL of .08%+, the driving is considered driving under the influence of alcohol. The driver may have a blood sample taken for a toxicology report to prove impairment. Officers are required to get a toxicology report before any medical treatment to prove impairment by drugs.
2. Officers may revoke licenses immediately for 7 highway code infractions/misdemeanors in a day period. Examples: Illegal Parking, Speeding.
3. Officers may revoke licenses immediately if 10 separate incidents of Misdemeanors or infractions committed through the usage of a vehicle occur within 7 days. Examples: Evading, Theft of a Motor Vehicle, Hit and Run.
4. Officers may revoke licenses immediately if you have 5 unpaid citations.
5. If a driver is consistently showing that they are a risk to themselves as well as the general populace, Officers can put in a request for revocation to the judiciary.

### Section 3 – Restoration

In all cases, the driver must not be committing vehicle related infractions or misdemeanors during the suspension period.

1. For DUI, 14 day wait, and pay an amount determined by the judiciary.
2. For other suspensions, 7 day wait, and pay an amount determined by the judiciary.

## STATUTE – The Justice Act

**THE JUSTICE ACT – 01/01/2026**

### Section 1 – The Law Enforcement Agencies of San Andreas

This section establishes the State Law Enforcement Agencies and their jurisdictions.

* Blaine County Sheriff's Office – Statewide criminal prosecution
* Los Santos Police Department – Statewide criminal prosecution

The Blaine County Sheriff's Office shall be headed by an elected Sheriff, who holds office in 5 month terms. The Los Santos Police Department is headed by the Chief of Police, who is appointed and removed by the Governor.

### Section 1.1 – Law Enforcement Agency Attorneys

The Law Enforcement Agencies of San Andreas may turn in evidence, and attorney(s) of their department. Attorneys within the office may pursue criminal charges on behalf of the State. They may also defend the agency they are hired under in civil cases. Attorneys hired may not represent individuals in lawsuits for which the agency is not a defendant.

Agency attorneys must be made special prosecutors by the Governor to defend the state interests in civil proceedings or to sue on behalf of the State.

### Section 2 – The Office of the Supreme Court

This State of San Andreas hereby recognizes the Office of the Supreme Court as the primary administrative body of the Judiciary, headed by the Justices to carry out all the duties prescribed to the Judiciary within the constitution.

### Section 2.1 – Bailiffs

Under the Office of the Supreme Court, Bailiffs shall be hired to maintain and protect the court. Bailiffs are granted full law enforcement authority within the offices and courtrooms of the Judiciary. This authority extends to the surrounding structure and lands of said offices and courtrooms. Bailiffs shall defer to Law Enforcement Officers of the LSPD and BCSO when the issue is serious in nature, or otherwise with appropriate cause for the Law Enforcement Officer to carry out their duties.

### Section 2.2 – Oversight of Criminal Defense

This Office of the Supreme Court is responsible for ensuring individuals have their right to a lawyer met, and lawyers are paid for work where they provide defense for individuals in cases. Individual lawyers are free to simultaneously provide both public and private criminal defense services, and provide civil litigation services. All criminal defense lawyers are independent of state agency's beyond the mandatory BAR. The Judiciary of San Andreas determines the payment rate based on case for state appointed attorneys.

State appointed attorneys are provided if available. Defendants refusal to accept a state appointed attorney may result in extended delays for trial.
