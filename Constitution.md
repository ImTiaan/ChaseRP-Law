# Constitution of San Andreas

We the people of the State of San Andreas commit our steadfast beliefs in the most righteous and necessary civil liberties to ordain this constitution into law for the securement and perpetuation thereof.

---

## Article 1  
### San Andreas Bill of Rights

### Section 1: The People’s Government
Government is instituted for the security, benefit and protection of the people, in whom all political power is inherent, together with the right to alter, modify or reform government whenever required by the public good. The right to vote shall not be infringed for persons of age determined by state law except in the case of Felony conviction without expungement or pardon.

### Section 2: Right to a Trial by Jury
The right of trial by jury shall extend to all misdemeanors and felonies at law without regard to the amount in controversy. The right to trial by jury shall also extend to the defendants of civil complaints whereupon relief sought by the plaintiff is solely monetary. A jury trial may be waived by the parties in all cases to effectuate a speedy trial. A duly appointed judge shall take place as the trier of fact should a jury be unavailable or waived.

### Section 3: Right to a Bench Trial
The right to a bench trial for all crimes not requiring confinement until trial shall not be infringed when available. Confinement until trial shall only extend to the most severe of criminal prosecutions.

### Section 4: Right to redress of injuries or wrongs
Every person is entitled to a certain remedy in the laws for all injuries or wrongs which he may receive to his person, property or character, and to obtain justice freely and without purchase, completely and without denial, promptly and without delay, conformable to the laws.

### Section 5: Right against imprisonment for debt
No person shall be imprisoned for obtaining civil debt alone within San Andreas.

### Section 6: Subordination of the Military
The military is subordinate to civil power and commanded by the elected or appointed civilian representatives of the people. Military personnel shall defer to state authorities for the prosecution and confinement based upon state law. Habeas corpus may not be suspended unless required by public safety in cases of rebellion or invasion. No standing army shall be maintained in this state in times of peace.

### Section 7: Rights against subversions of Due Process
A bill of attainder may not be passed into law. No person shall be subject to penalties or prosecution of a law passed ex post facto.

### Section 8: Right to Bail
A person shall be released on bail by sufficient sureties, unless charged with felony offenses when the facts are evident or the presumption great, and the person’s release would cause substantial likelihood of great bodily harm to another person or flight from justice.

### Section 9: Right against Depraved Contractual Obligations
No contractual obligation may result in a person losing life, being forced to commit a crime, or doing harm to body or personal property. No contract shall reprimand a person to prison or make him insecure in his person. No contract shall deprive someone of their ability to report illegal activity.

### Section 10: Rights Unenumerated
The enumeration in the United States Constitution and the State of San Andreas, of certain rights, shall not be construed to deny or disparage others retained by the people.

---

## Article 2  
### The State of San Andreas

This state shall be called the state of San Andreas and shall consist of and have jurisdiction over the territory embraced in the act of Congress entitled. The State of San Andreas is an inseparable part of the United States of America, and the United States Constitution is the supreme law of the land.

---

## Article 3  
### Division of Powers and Limited Immunity

The powers of government shall be divided into two distinct departments: Executive and Judicial. No person or persons belonging to or constituting one of these departments shall exercise any of the powers properly belonging to either of the others except in the instances expressly provided in this constitution. All State Officials shall be subject to the criminal penalties of the following regardless of other allowed immunities while acting within or outside the scope of their duties.

1. Corruption or misuse of office for personal gain  
2. Participating in Treason  
3. Perjury within Court  
4. Committing Prosecutorial Misconduct  
5. Participating in Bribery  

Any conviction named thereof shall immediately remove the individual from office. Any conviction of a felony within office shall remove said person from office, but they may be eligible to run for the office again unless impeached. All State Officials are subject to the laws of San Andreas during acts outside the scope of their duties. These enumerated exceptions do not limit the powers of Impeachment, nor does the process of Impeachment limit the ability for the State to prosecute officials for the enumerated reasons.

---

## Article 4  
### The Executive Branch

### Section 1: Officers and Appointment
The executive branch is headed by the Governor of San Andreas. The Governor of San Andreas, and their Lieutenant Governor, shall be elected on a shared ballot by a plurality of the citizens of San Andreas every three months.

### Section 2: Powers and Duties of the Chief Executive
The Governor shall see that the law is faithfully executed. He shall fill any vacancy that may occur in the offices of the Executive or Supreme Court, until such a time the original term for those offices ends and appointments made through normal means. The Governor is commander in chief of the state’s armed forces. The Governor may call it forth to execute the laws when no other reasonable means remains, suppress insurrection, and repel invasion. The Governor holds sole pardon authority of state originated convictions. The Governor may not pardon themselves pre-emptive nor post hoc of a criminal conviction. The Governor holds the power to hire prosecutors to prosecute specific criminal and civil cases, as well as defend the State, as the need arises. These special prosecutors can not supersede a prosecutor originally assigned to criminal or civil proceedings. The Governor holds the power of the Executive Bill, to pass a new legislative bill without judicial approval and outside of the original authority proscribed to the Governor. An Executive Bill may be signed into law a maximum of five times per term. These bills are still subject to judicial review. These bills may be repealed by a governor without judicial approval as well. Executive Bills must be of a single subject.

---

## Article 5  
### The Judiciary

### Section 1: Structure
The Judiciary of San Andreas shall be the head of the sole State jurisdiction for all laws of San Andreas, composed of a trial court and the Supreme Court of San Andreas. The Supreme Court of San Andreas is headed by the authority of three Justices, and is the highest court of San Andreas. The Justices shall be responsible for hiring and firing any clerks managing the state courts. A single Justice is appointed by the Governor once per the Governor’s term. The appointed Justice serves for nine months, at which time they will be up for re-election as a judge. A judge may be re-appointed by the governor to serve another nine months as Justice if they are eligible as a judge after re-election.

The trial court has original jurisdiction in all civil and criminal cases. The trial courts shall be composed of five elected judges. A judge shall serve for six months before being up for re-election.

The Supreme Court shall be granted the ability to hear all appeals of its choice and hold all appellate authority. The Supreme Court has the choice to garner original jurisdiction for all cases pertaining to Article 3.

All Justices and Judges shall not hold any other office under the United States or San Andreas, nor shall they act as a magistrate while running for election of any state office, other than re-election of a judicial seat.

### Section 2: Powers and Duties
The Courts of San Andreas shall hold sole judicial authority over criminal and civil complaints within San Andreas. The Courts of San Andreas shall also be trier of fact for all complaints arising for the San Andreas Armed Forces. The Supreme Court of San Andreas shall derive the rules of court procedure and evidence, pursuant to laws expanding or limiting the court’s processes.

The Courts of San Andreas have the power of judicial review, to hold the supreme law of the lands within its jurisdiction above new or old laws. Entire laws, or portions of laws, may be ruled to be in violation of superior law.

The Courts of San Andreas have the power to define the punishment or mercy granted for crimes committed, at no more or no less than the law allows if specified. When unspecified, the judiciary shall hold the power to define the appropriate penalty.

The Courts of San Andreas may find persons who testify within their jurisdiction guilty of perjury during trial proceedings. This finding is still subject to the due process granted by appellate review and executive pardon.

The Supreme Court may hire and appoint Bailiffs, Clerks, and other court personnel to carry out the duties of the Judiciary.

The Supreme Court shall preside over the San Andreas State Bar. The State Bar shall define and enforce mandatory qualifications and codes of conduct for all legal professionals practicing within the Courts of San Andreas, in conjunction with requirements defined by law. No law or Bar requirement shall infringe upon an individual’s right to represent themselves pro se in court for any proceeding.

---

## Article 6  
### Legislation

Legislative Bills are presented to the trial judges by Bar certified sponsors or the Office of the Governor. Bills shall be initially approved by a majority of Judges to send to the Supreme Court for consideration. Should the Supreme Court approve the bill, the bill must be signed or vetoed by the Governor.

---

## Article 7  
### Impeachment

All duly elected judges and state officials are subject to impeachment. An attorney may file for Articles of Impeachment and submit them to the Judges of San Andreas. Impeachment may occur for any section described in Article 3, abuse of authority, corruption, conduct unbecoming, gross derelictions of duty, or other high crimes and misdemeanors. The Judges of San Andreas vote by majority to present the Articles to the Supreme Court, which shall then call for a hearing. Should a majority of the Justices find that official guilty of the Articles, that official is removed from office. Impeached officials lose sovereign immunity and may never again be elected to office.
